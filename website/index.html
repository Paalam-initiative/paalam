Paalam

Malayalam for "Bridge"


A youth-led initiative dedicated to dismantling the culture of silence and systemic stigma surrounding menstrual hygiene in India.

Discover Our Work

Our Vision

We act as the vital connector between deep-rooted silence and the essential knowledge required for menstrual health and dignity. Our goal is to transform menstruation from a source of shame into a recognized standard of health and public dignity.

By operating at the intersection of education, equity, and advocacy, we bridge the gap between deep-seated taboos and essential reproductive knowledge.

The Challenge

In many regions across India—particularly within rural and underserved communities—menstruation remains shrouded in a "culture of silence."

When the topic is taboo, knowledge remains inaccessible, leading to improper hygiene practices, school dropouts, and long-term health complications. We are faced with a critical question:

If not us, who? If not now, when?

The Three-Pillar Framework

To create lasting systemic change, Paalam operates through a multi-dimensional approach to transform the landscape of menstrual health.

1. Community-Centric Education

The Knowledge Bridge

We facilitate immersive awareness sessions in spaces often overlooked by mainstream healthcare, including schools, orphanages, and old-age homes. By engaging in intergenerational dialogue, we don't just teach biology; we challenge the cultural narratives that sustain stigma.

2. Resource Equity & Distribution

The Access Bridge

Awareness is ineffective without the means to act. We lead strategic fundraising campaigns to procure and distribute sanitary products to those in need. Our focus is on ensuring that "period poverty" never stands in the way of a person’s education or daily life.

3. Narrative Shifting & Digital Advocacy

The Awareness Bridge

Through high-impact digital campaigns and storytelling, we bring the conversation into the public square. By leveraging social media to share facts, dismantle myths, and amplify voices, we are building a digital ecosystem where menstrual health is discussed openly and without apology.
